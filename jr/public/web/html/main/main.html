Spider sense is tingling

Creation: Spider-Man was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko. His first appearance was in the comic book Amazing Fantasy #15, released in August 1962.

Iconic teenager: Unlike most superheroes of the time, Spider-Man was portrayed as an ordinary teenager, facing everyday problems while trying to balance his personal life and responsibilities as a superhero.

Secret identity: Peter Parker's secret identity as Spider-Man was one of the first times a superhero had to deal with the challenge of hiding his true identity to protect those he loved.

Iconic villains: Spider-Man has one of the most popular rogues' galleries in comics, including the Green Goblin, Venom, Doctor Octopus, Carnage, and others.

Web-slingers: Spider-Man doesn't have the natural ability to produce webs but uses web-shooters that he invented himself. However, in some versions of the story, Spider-Man acquires the ability to produce organic webs.
